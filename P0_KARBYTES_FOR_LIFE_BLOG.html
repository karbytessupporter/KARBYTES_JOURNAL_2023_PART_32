Today I uploaded a video of me playing an acoustic guitar song which follows a chord progression pattern which I invented back in 2008 (so the song has a particular nostalgic quality which is unlike the other guitar songs I am featuring in my SOUND_TRACK_LOOP_COUNTER software application tutorial web page on my primary website). I plan on using today’s guitar recording as one of the four guitar sound tracks for the aforementioned web page. Other sound tracks which are included on that web page are a recording of frogs croaking and a recording of me playing the (acoustic) drums. (I think the three guitar songs which I recorded before today’s guitar song recording fit the theme of my brand better, but I decided to include today’s song in my primary website because it feels like it ties together two separate eras of my life: the era back when I felt relatively “pure” and “innocent” and “relaxed” and the era afterwards when I felt more like some thug and ostracized person being drafted into a war and having to deal with more seedy, stressful, and political subject matter). My favorite guitar song in that collection of four guitar songs is karbytes_guitar_07_june_2023.mp3 because it best captures the spirit of my Summer Camp by Karbytes experience. When I listen to that song, it evokes mental imagery of the mountains, trees, and traveling through dark and scary passages and the transitions between night and day and vice versa. For me, that song has a fun and adventurous feel. It’s not something I can easily repeat (like karbytes_guitar_13_may_2023.mp3) because those songs were improvised and not “muscle memory” songs like the other two guitar songs.
