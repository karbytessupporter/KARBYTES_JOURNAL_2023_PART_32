One of my goals (as controversial as it seems) is not relying on a petroleum powered vehicle (especially one I don’t own) as my primary means of transportation. Hence, I strive to continue only relying on ecologically sustainable and “communist” means of transportation to get between my living area and my future place of (paid (and tax paying)) employment by walking, bicycling, or riding BART trains. (I hope to own a fully electric car someday such as the (two-seater and 100% electric) Smart Car and, when I do travel outside the San Francisco Bay Area, I am open to other transportation means such as airplane, train, taxis, and car rental).

I am attempting to save up money for a cheap (mechanical (i.e. non-motorized (i.e. 100% human-powered))) bicycle to order to pick up in store from Amazon dot Com to have delivered to my house within the next two months. (I saw that I will have to manually assemble the bicycle if it is delivered. That’s fine. I don’t mind doing that part myself. It’s hundreds of dollars cheaper to order a mountain bike from Amazon than to buy a pre-assembled mountain bike from a specialty bike store. Also, pre-assembling my own bike is a good learning experience and something I should know how to do as someone who intends to heavily use a bike to get around. I will have to become my own bike mechanic so that I am not too dependent on other people to get around. Finally, I saw (adult) mountain bikes for sale on Amazon dot Com which are priced at less than $200).
