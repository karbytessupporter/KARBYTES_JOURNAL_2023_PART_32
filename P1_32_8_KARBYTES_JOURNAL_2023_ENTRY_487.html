I figured out how to adjust my backpack such that it now properly fits me with its weight mostly distributed to my hips instead of to my shoulders (which is something I think I “should have” figured out within hours of acquiring that backpack). It’s a Teton brand backpack which I ordered from Amazon dot Com and which cost me less than $100. So far, the backpack seems to be a very sturdy and functional for my needs (particularly for carrying my clothes, sleeping bag, laptop, and other gear including a tent which can be strapped to the bottom of the pack). Now that I figured out how to adjust the backpack straps to fit me correctly, I think I will be less sore and tired using that backpack for now on. (I used the ten-second self-timer camera feature on my Samsung Galaxy Android phone while that phone was resting against my tent case to take the photograph which is featured on this web page. I also uploaded that image to my GitHub account and shared that image as a post on my Instagram account).
